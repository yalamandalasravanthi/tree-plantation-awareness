BENIFITS OF CLEANLINESS

Improved Public Health

One of the primary goals of Swachh Bharat is to promote cleanliness and hygiene across India, particularly in rural and urban areas. By improving sanitation facilities, promoting handwashing, and ensuring proper waste management, the mission aims to reduce the spread of diseases such as diarrhea, cholera, and vector-borne illnesses.

Environmental Impact

The campaign also focuses on cleanliness beyond just personal hygiene. It aims to clean up streets, public spaces, and water bodies, thereby reducing pollution and improving the environment. Proper waste management practices such as segregation, recycling, and disposal help in preserving natural resources and maintaining ecological balance.

Boost to Tourism and Economy

A cleaner environment and better sanitation facilities contribute to a positive image of India globally. Clean cities and tourist spots attract more visitors, boosting tourism revenues. Additionally, improved sanitation and waste management can lead to job creation in sectors related to cleanliness and environmental services, thus contributing to economic growth.

About Us

Swachh Bharat Abhiyan, launched on October 2, 2014, by Prime Minister Narendra Modi, is India's largest cleanliness drive aimed at making India cleaner and more hygienic. The mission's primary goals include eliminating open defecation, promoting sanitation facilities, and ensuring proper waste management across urban and rural India. It operates under two sub-missions: Swachh Bharat Mission (Gramin) for rural areas and Swachh Bharat Mission (Urban) for cities. The initiative emphasizes behavioral change, encouraging people to adopt cleanliness habits and dispose of waste responsibly. It has mobilized millions of citizens, celebrities, and government officials to participate in cleanliness drives, awareness campaigns, and toilet construction activities.
